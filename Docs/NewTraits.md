As for additional traits, considering the Big 5 personality model, there are several possibilities that come to mind:

1. **Outgoing vs. Reserved**: While we have traits that might make a pawn more social (e.g., "Kind"), it could be useful to have explicit traits for extroversion and introversion. An "Outgoing" pawn could receive mood boosts from socializing, while a "Reserved" pawn might prefer solitude.

2. **Adventurous vs. Cautious**: This could correspond to the Openness dimension of the Big 5. An "Adventurous" pawn might be more likely to explore, and enjoy trying new things, while a "Cautious" pawn prefers familiar activities and places.

3. **Relaxed vs. High-Strung**: Relating to the Neuroticism dimension, a "Relaxed" pawn could be less likely to have mental breaks, while a "High-Strung" pawn might be more susceptible.

4. **Organized vs. Spontaneous**: This could relate to the Conscientiousness dimension. An "Organized" pawn might work more efficiently or keep their surroundings tidier, while a "Spontaneous" pawn is more likely to abandon tasks or leave things messy.

5. **Empathetic vs. Self-Centered**: This could relate to the Agreeableness dimension. An "Empathetic" pawn might get mood boosts from helping others and have better social interactions, while a "Self-Centered" pawn might be more likely to engage in selfish behaviors.

Remember, the goal isn't to label every possible personality trait, but to create a range of behaviors that feel distinct and meaningful. And of course, different traits can interact in interesting ways. A "Kind" pawn who is also "Self-Centered" might be friendly but not very helpful, for example. The possibilities are endless!
